Health ECGO

Market fit for Ecgo, a new cardiac health device

Ecgo  ·  Health Tech  ·  2018–2019

#UX Research  ·  #TechDriven  ·  #DataScience  ·  #Telemedicine  ·  #PublicHealth  ·  #Startup

A device without software.

Ecgo had developed a new cardiac Holter sensor — but had no digital product around it. I was brought in to understand patients, doctors, and medical technicians deeply enough to design software from scratch that could work in hospitals, clinics, and patients' homes.

Field Research

01 / Objective

Our objective as the Solunova team was to understand all actors and their context of usage of a Holter cardiac exam in public health institutions.

Research Questions

How does the traditional cardiac Holter flow work?

Who is part of this flow, what characterizes them?

What is the minimum service (SAL) that is necessary to be implemented correctly?

What are the challenges and opportunities of implementing software for patients?

The Plan

02 / Planning

My research approach was qualitative, with the commercial side completing the findings by using market, data-driven information.

This qualitative approach was based off of two research activities: non-participant observation sessions and unstructured interviews.

Participants

4 patients
2 medical technicians
2 cardiac medical specialists

03 / Conducting

I conducted non-participant observations and in-depth interviews across three key user groups — in hospitals, clinics, and patients' homes.

01 Cardiologists

Experts who shaped the cardiac exam report — defining the data quality doctors need to make clinical decisions.

02 Medical Technicians

Key to understanding how cardiac signals travel from the device to the cardiologist — the critical handoff point.

03 Patients

Segmented by age, tech comfort, and experience with the Holter process — the widest range of needs on the journey.

Field Observation

Non-participant observation carried out in 2-hour sessions at three institutions — tracking the full Holter exam flow firsthand.

Vival Medical Center  ·  Kaplan Cardiac Foundation  ·  Hospital Cordillera Oriente

04 / Concluding

Our research validated the device's potential — and gave us the foundation to design a completely new kind of cardiac monitoring service.

7 Days

Continuous cardiac monitoring — 4× longer than the traditional Holter exam.

Key Finding

Detected cardiac conditions the traditional 24-hour Holter exam missed entirely.

Key Output

Co-designed the clinical report and Service Level Agreement alongside the cardiologist and data scientist.

What We Built

05 / Implementing

Dashboard for Cardiologists

Add and register medical supervisors, patients, studies (reports), real-time exams, and new devices. Works for desktop and tablet.

APP for Patients

Users can input symptoms and log activity during their heart exam. Real-time data complements the cardiac signal monitoring.

The product we built

Real impact

The implementation was carried out through laboratory test demos, generating real medical exams with continued iterations until a stable version was reached. Design was key — considering accessibility for patients and adequate visualization of reports for cardiologists.

Still In Use 2019

Implemented at the Kaplan Foundation.
