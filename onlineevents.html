Events THE PLATFORM

Internalize an external product: How does the UX/UI adapt?

#UI  ·  #DesignSystem  ·  #Collaboration

The Context

Studio. Acquired,
but not yet integrated.

The Virtual Event Platform is a company providing software solutions focused on event management. Studio — a product acquired by the Platform — is an audiovisual production tool designed to optimize virtual and hybrid events through video conferencing.

My main challenge was ensuring Studio became an integral part of the Virtual Event Platform experience — fluid and without interruptions for both attendees and event organizers.

Studio Challenges

01 Old Legacy Design System 4 YRS without design system updates

Structural deficiencies, outdated standards, and failed integrations made Studio's path into the Platform a significant obstacle.

02 Technological Limitation 0% accessibility compliance at integration

Polymer components didn't align with behavior, look, or accessibility standards — making a direct integration technically impossible.

How did I approach it?

1 Audit our features and components

Identify and prioritize essential components within the Studio experience.

2 Reverse engineering

Understand the construction of components — states, structure, and behavior.

3 Design & iteration

Apply atomic design to rebuild components consistently and accessibly, aligned with the Platform's standards.

4 Publication

Advocate the library to product and dev teams with documentation, cheat sheets, and business impact.

2 / Reverse engineering

The investigation focused on understanding the construction of Carina's components. These were highly intricate, requiring significant design and development resources, and designed for use cases that often differed from Studio's limited set.

This phase concluded with a comprehensive understanding of how UI, states, content, and accessibility were structured and functioned within Carina's components.
